Help

A gentle guide to retirement planning

🧑‍🏫

Welcome — you’re in the right place

If retirement planning feels confusing, that’s normal. This guide explains what the screens mean and how to make decisions without needing finance jargon.

A simple learning path

1
Set a baseline

Enter your best‑estimate inputs. Save this as “Baseline”.

2
Check Stress tests

If Stress fails, treat the plan as fragile even if the projection looks good.

3
Early retirement? Use Bridge

Bridge checks the gap to State Pension age and shows when the pot might run out.

4
Add uncertainty with Monte Carlo

Use Success %, Worst‑case depletion age, and P10 pot to understand downside risk.

5
Compare scenarios

Save 2–3 options and compare side‑by‑side. Prefer plans with margin for error.

6
Improve with small levers

Use Top 3 levers to move the weakest area from Amber/Red to Green.

What this tool is (and isn’t)

  • It is a planning tool that tests resilience (margin for error).
  • It isn’t a prediction of the future or financial advice.
  • Values are shown in today’s money (inflation‑adjusted).
  • Focus on robust plans, not perfect precision.

The retirement problem (plain words)

  • Time: you may live longer than expected.
  • Bad luck: markets don’t rise smoothly.
  • Spending: drawing too much too early can cause trouble.
  • Sequence risk: poor returns early hurt the most.

Plain‑English explanations (tap to expand)

What is Monte Carlo?▾
It runs your plan many times using random market return paths. It doesn’t predict the future — it shows how often your plan still works under uncertainty.
What does “Success probability” mean?▾
The % of simulated futures where your pot does not hit the “ruin” rule (usually £0) before the end age.
What is P10 terminal pot?▾
A pessimistic outcome. 9 out of 10 futures end with more pot than this. It’s a helpful downside buffer.
What are Stress tests?▾
Specific “bad timing” checks like a crash at retirement or a run of poor early returns. Passing stress tests means resilience.
What is the Bridge?▾
If you retire before State Pension age, the Bridge is the gap you must fund yourself. The Bridge tab checks whether the pot can support that period.
What does “today’s money” mean?▾
Values are inflation‑adjusted. £30k means £30k of buying power today, not future cash. This makes scenarios easier to compare.

How to make decisions (simple rules)

Can I retire earlier?

Check Bridge (gap holds), Stress (gatekeeper), and Monte Carlo (uncertainty).

Can I spend more?

Watch minimum pot after drawing starts and P10 pot. Avoid “knife‑edge” plans.

Which scenario is better?

Prefer margin for error: higher robustness even if income is slightly lower.

Common worries

“What if my assumptions are wrong?”▾
That’s normal. Use Stress and Monte Carlo to see sensitivity. If tiny changes cause failure, the plan is fragile.
“Why do tabs disagree?”▾
Projection is an average path. Stress is specific bad timing. Monte Carlo is many random futures. Each answers a different question.
“What should I do if I see Amber?”▾
Amber means “watch this area”. Use Top 3 levers: adjust retirement age, contributions, or spending. Small changes often move Amber to Green.
Teacher tip: Save 3 scenarios (Baseline, Early retirement, Lower spend). Compare them side‑by‑side to see trade‑offs clearly.
